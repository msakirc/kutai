# Crisis Comms Playbook — Tier 1: Brand Misstep / Pile-On

**Trigger:** A tweet, post, or statement went wrong and is drawing negative attention.
Examples: insensitive copy, tone-deaf announcement, misread audience reaction, pile-on.

**Target window:** Respond within 2–4 hours of first signal.

---

## Immediate actions (first 30 min)

1. **Assess blast radius.** Count shares / replies / quote-tweets. Is this growing or plateauing?
2. **Do not delete silently.** Deleting without acknowledgment almost always amplifies. Decide: defend, acknowledge, or delete-with-apology.
3. **Draft holding statement.** Use the variants from `crisis/draft_holding`. Post the least-defensive, most-human one.
4. **Pause scheduled content.** `/crisis open 1 <product>` triggers freeze on A2/B1 sends. Confirm freeze is active.
5. **Monitor cadence.** Set 30-min check-in until sentiment stops growing.

## Holding statement shape (Tier 1)

```
We got this wrong. [One sentence: what happened, no excuses.]
We're sorry — [who was affected] deserved better.
[What we're changing / learning.] We'll [next step].
```

Keep it under 280 characters for tweet-native delivery.

## Recovery path

- Apology post (founder-authored; agent drafts, founder edits).
- Follow-up acknowledgment at 24h showing what changed.
- Internal retro: what process broke that allowed this through?

## Decision card

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| <200 impressions, plateau | Monitor only; no public statement needed |
| >200 impressions, growing | Draft + post holding statement |
| Verified false accusation | Respond with facts; no apology needed |
| Involves protected group | Escalate to Tier 2 if brand damage is material |

## Escalation triggers

- Verified press coverage → escalate to Tier 2.
- Legal threat received → skip to Tier 3.
- Existential narrative (fraud / shutdown) → Tier 4.

---

*Founder authors the final post. Agent drafts, monitors, and reminds.*
